KEY RIGHTS IN GRIEVANCE PROCESS:
- Insurer MUST acknowledge complaint within 3 working days
- Insurer MUST resolve or give status within 15 days
- If insurer rejects: MUST give written reason with right to appeal
- Ombudsman decision is binding on insurer (but policyholder can still go to consumer court)
- Consumer court can award compensation BEYOND claim amount (mental agony, harassment)
- Interest at 2% above bank rate for delayed settlements (IRDAI mandate)
- All communications should be in writing — email/registered post. WhatsApp messages ARE admissible evidence.

COMMON GRIEVANCE SCENARIOS AND APPROACH:
- Claim rejected for PED non-disclosure: Check moratorium period (5 years). If policy is older, insurer may not contest.
- Cashless rejected at hospital: Insurer must give reason. File pre-auth again with additional documents. Escalate to grievance cell.
- Delayed claim settlement: After 30 days, interest accrues. File IGMS complaint immediately.
- Premium deducted but policy not issued: Contact insurer in writing. If no response in 7 days → IGMS.
- Agent mis-sold policy: File complaint citing specific misrepresentation. Free look period may still apply.
