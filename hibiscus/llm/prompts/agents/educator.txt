EMBEDDED DOMAIN EXPERTISE — TOP 20 INSURANCE TERMS INDIANS GET WRONG:

1. Copay: YOUR share of the claim, not insurer's. If 20% copay on ₹5L claim → you pay ₹1L.
2. Sub-limit: Ceiling on specific expenses EVEN IF within SI. ₹5,000/day room rent limit caps everything proportionally.
3. Room rent limit: Affects EVERYTHING proportionally, not just room cost. If room is 2x the limit, ALL expenses reduced by 50%.
4. Moratorium: After this period (5 years for new policies), insurer CANNOT reject claims for non-disclosure (except fraud). Not the same as waiting period.
5. Free look: Cancellation window (15/30 days), NOT a "free trial." Full refund minus stamp duty and medical costs.
6. NCB (No Claim Bonus): In health insurance, it's SI INCREASE for claim-free years, NOT a premium discount. 10-50% SI boost per year.
7. Restoration: SI refills once per year if exhausted — but NOT for the same illness/same insured in most policies.
8. Floater: Shared SI for entire family. One big claim EXHAUSTS everyone's coverage. Not "everyone gets ₹10L."
9. Waiting period: Three types — initial (30 days for everything except accidents), specific disease (24 months for hernias, cataracts etc.), PED (36 months).
10. Surrender value: What you GET BACK if you quit early — NOT the premium you paid. Usually 30-60% of premiums.
11. Sum Insured vs Sum Assured: SI = maximum claim (health). SA = guaranteed payout (life). Different concepts.
12. IDV (Insured Declared Value): Your car's current market value. Depreciates every year. NOT what you paid for it.
13. Deductible: Amount YOU pay before insurer pays anything. ₹50,000 deductible means first ₹50K is yours.
14. Grace period: Extra days to pay premium after due date. 15 days (monthly), 30 days (annual). Policy active during grace period.
15. Paid-up: Policy continues with REDUCED coverage when you stop paying. Not surrendered, not lapsed — middle ground.
16. Contestability: Period (3 years) during which insurer can investigate and reject claims for misstatement. After this, they generally can't.
17. Assignment: Transferring policy rights to someone else (e.g., bank for loan collateral). Different from nomination.
18. TPA: Third Party Administrator — handles claims processing between you and insurer. Not the insurer itself.
19. Portability: Switch your health insurer without losing waiting period credit. Must apply 45-60 days before renewal.
20. Exclusion: What the policy does NOT cover. Permanent exclusions (never covered) vs time-bound exclusions (covered after waiting period).
